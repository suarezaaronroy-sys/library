Grimoire 008 - Search System - June 2026

SEO 101: Old Search, New Search, And The AI Answer Engine Era

Search did not die. It moved the finish line. The page that used to rank first now has to survive being cited, summarized, and skipped in the same breath — all by an AI that may never send the click. This guide covers the fundamentals that still run everything underneath, the new rules for AI answer engines, and a full VA playbook for running the whole thing operationally. Read it once. Then use the SOPs in Part VI to run it.

Part I
The Shift
Why SEO is not dead, but the old mental model is too small
M1 - M4
M1
The SEO Storm Map
Search is becoming an answer layer, not only a results page

The game used to be easy to describe. You had a page. Someone had a query. Google made the match. The details were complicated — crawling, canonicals, backlinks, page speed — but the interface was not. A user typed something. Google returned ten blue links. Your page competed for positions one through ten. That mental model still matters. But it is no longer the whole game.

Now the search engine sometimes just answers. Google AI Overviews and AI Mode summarize and cite. ChatGPT search browses and quotes. Copilot blends assistant logic with web retrieval. Users ask longer, messier questions and get synthesized answers — not ten links and a "good luck." The referral is no longer guaranteed.

Old SEO asked: how do I rank? New SEO asks: how do I become the source an answer engine reaches for when it needs to sound credible?

This does not mean technical SEO, links, keywords, and page quality stop mattering. It means they become the floor. The ceiling is now source-worthiness: original facts, entity clarity, evidence, topical depth, structured information, brand trust, and content that can be safely cited without a human reading the whole site first.

M2
Old SEO vs New SEO
The mental model upgrade
Old SEONew SEO
Rank for keywordsBe cited for questions, comparisons, entities, tasks, and decisions

NOTE
Google says there are no special extra requirements to appear in AI Overviews or AI Mode beyond Search eligibility and standard SEO best practices. That is true technically. Strategically, the bar for being cited is different from the bar for merely being indexed.
M3
Why Search Is Changing
The query is becoming a conversation

Old search was patient. A user typed "best CRM for dentists," clicked four tabs, compared options, watched a YouTube review, asked Reddit, lost a weekend, then booked a demo. AI search skips most of that. One prompt: "compare GoHighLevel, HubSpot, and Pipedrive for a dental clinic with 3 locations and heavy SMS follow-up" — and the engine synthesizes an answer from sources it chose. Your page may or may not be one of them.

Old behavior

Many small searches

Users refine queries manually across pages and tabs.

New behavior

One complex prompt

Users ask for synthesis, tradeoffs, and recommendations.

Old result

Ranked links

The search engine points at pages and lets users evaluate.

New result

Summarized answer

The engine cites sources, pulls facts, and frames the decision.

This creates a new SEO job: your content must be useful to the answer construction process. A page full of vague claims is hard to cite. A page with clear definitions, tables, examples, data, authorship, and caveats is easier to use.

M4
What Still Works
The old fundamentals did not vanish

Search engines still need to crawl pages, index content, understand topics, evaluate quality, and decide what to show. AI search often depends on the same web substrate. If your site blocks crawlers, hides important content in inaccessible JavaScript, has no internal links, has weak titles, or provides thin content, AI will not magically rescue it.

Still matters
Crawlability, indexability, site architecture, page experience, internal links, titles, helpful content, structured data, reputation.
Less sufficient
Keyword stuffing, generic skyscraper content, empty listicles, AI rewrites of competitor posts, backlink-only strategies.
More important
Original evidence, first-hand expertise, entity clarity, page-level trust, answer-ready formatting, update discipline.
Part II
Old SEO Fundamentals

M5
Crawl: Can Search Engines Reach You?
Discovery comes before ranking

Crawling is the first gate. Search engines discover pages through links, sitemaps, feeds, submissions, and crawling systems. If your important pages are orphaned, blocked, too slow, hidden behind forms, or rendered in ways crawlers cannot access, they might as well not exist.

1

Submit XML sitemap

Include canonical URLs you care about. Keep it clean.

2

Check robots.txt

Do not accidentally block CSS, JavaScript, images, or important directories.

3

Use internal links

Every important page should be reachable from other relevant pages.

4

Inspect logs

See what bots actually crawl, not just what you hope they crawl.

M6
Index: Can Search Engines Store And Serve You?
Crawled is not the same as indexed

A page can be crawled but not indexed. Common reasons: duplication, canonical confusion, thin content, blocked snippets, noindex tags, low value, redirect issues, soft 404s, or content Google already has in better form elsewhere.

SignalWhat to check
CanonicalDoes the canonical URL point to the real preferred page?
NoindexAre meta robots or X-Robots-Tag headers blocking indexing?
DuplicationDoes this page add enough value beyond similar pages?
RenderingCan Google see the main content and links?
SnippetsIs the page eligible for snippets and supporting links?
AI
For Google AI features, pages need to be indexed and eligible to be shown in Search with a snippet. Blocking snippets may limit supporting-link eligibility.
M7
Keywords: Intent, Not Incantations
The keyword is a clue, not the strategy

Old keyword research often treated queries like magic phrases. Modern keyword work maps intent: what the user is trying to do, what they already know, what decision they are making, what format they need, and what proof would make them trust the answer.

Informational
What is local SEO? How does AI search work?
Commercial
Best CRM for med spas. GoHighLevel vs HubSpot.
Transactional
Book SEO consultation. Buy technical SEO audit.
Support
Fix indexing issue. Why did traffic drop?

AI search makes long-tail intent more valuable because users can ask naturally. Build pages around complete problems, not exact-match fragments.

M8
Site Architecture
Search engines understand relationships through structure

Architecture tells search engines what your site is about and which pages matter. A strong site has clear topical hubs, supporting pages, breadcrumbs, internal links, and URLs that humans can understand.

Simple topical structure

  /seo/local-seo/
  /seo/ai-search-optimization/

The point is not pretty folders. The point is semantic clarity. A crawler, a user, and an AI system should be able to infer the relationship between pages without detective work.

Mentions, reviews, citations, community references, author reputation, datasets, videos, podcasts, and third-party profiles all help machines and humans decide whether you are real.

M11
AEO, GEO, And The Naming Mess
Answer Engine Optimization and Generative Engine Optimization

The industry is still arguing about what to call this. AEO, GEO, LLMO, AI Search Optimization — pick one, they all point at the same shift. AEO usually means optimizing for answer engines: featured snippets, voice answers, People Also Ask, assistants, and direct answers. GEO usually means optimizing for generative engines: AI Overviews, AI Mode, ChatGPT search, Perplexity, Copilot, and systems that synthesize sources. The naming will settle. The underlying problem — how do you become the source an AI cites? — is the same regardless.

SEO
Help search engines crawl, index, understand, rank, and display your pages.
AEO
Structure content so answer systems can extract a clear response.
GEO
Make your site source-worthy for generative search and AI citations.
Reality
They overlap. The best strategy is useful content with technical access, entity clarity, evidence, and trust.
M12
Entity SEO
Machines need to know who and what you are

An entity is a thing with identity: a person, company, product, place, service, concept, event, or dataset. AI search is entity-hungry because it needs to connect facts. If your brand, authors, products, services, locations, and expertise are ambiguous, you make the machine work harder.

Brand entity

Who are you?

Name, logo, about page, founders, locations, social profiles, organization schema.

Author entity

Who wrote this?

Author bios, credentials, reviewed-by fields, publication history.

Service entity

What do you do?

Clear service pages, examples, pricing context, FAQs, case studies.

Place entity

Where do you operate?

Business Profile, local pages, consistent NAP, reviews, local proof.

M13
Information Gain
Generic content is invisible in a world full of summaries
Field note
I audited a client's blog once — 70 posts on the same topic cluster. Every one of them said the same six things. Not because the writer was bad. Because the brief never asked for original thinking. When AI Overviews started covering that topic, every single post became invisible. The one that survived: it had client data, a named framework, and a decision tree we built from actual client calls. That post still gets cited. The other 69 do not.

If your page says what every other page says, AI can summarize the topic without you. Your job is to contribute something the model and user cannot get everywhere else: original data, field experience, screenshots, tests, benchmarks, opinions with reasoning, case studies, decision trees, and examples from actual work.

Weak contentStronger content
"SEO is important for businesses.""In our 42-location service audit, pages with service + city + proof blocks converted 31% higher than generic city pages."
"Use structured data.""Here is the LocalBusiness + Service schema pattern we use, and when we avoid FAQ schema."
"AI search is changing SEO.""Here are 12 prompts where ChatGPT cites competitors but not us, and the content gaps causing it."
RULE
If a page could be generated by summarizing the top five results, it is not strong enough. Add evidence that came from your actual work.
M14
Citation Design
Make your pages easy to quote, verify, and trust

AI answer engines need facts they can safely attach to sources. You can help by designing pages with citation-friendly blocks: concise definitions, tables, statistics with methodology, step-by-step processes, pros/cons, dates, version notes, and clear claims.

1

State the claim

Use precise language. Avoid mushy marketing claims.

2

Show the evidence

Data, screenshots, examples, sources, test conditions, or author experience.

3

Explain the caveat

Tell readers where the claim applies and where it does not.

4

Format for extraction

Use tables, bullets, definitions, and descriptive headings without stripping personality.

M16
AI Crawlers And Robots.txt
Search inclusion, training use, and user-triggered browsing are different

AI discovery now involves more than Googlebot. OpenAI documents separate crawlers for search, training, ads validation, and user-triggered browsing. These controls are not the same. Blocking a training crawler is different from blocking a search crawler that may surface your site in ChatGPT search answers.

Bot/controlWhat it affectsStrategic note
GooglebotGoogle Search crawling and indexingUsually allow for SEO visibility.
Google-ExtendedControls use for some Google generative AI model training/grounding use casesSeparate from normal Google Search crawling.
OAI-SearchBotChatGPT search result inclusionAllow if you want to appear in ChatGPT search answers.
GPTBotOpenAI model training crawlBlock or allow based on your content policy.
ChatGPT-UserUser-triggered page visitsRobots.txt may not apply because the user initiated the action.

This is not legal advice. It is operational hygiene. Decide deliberately which systems can access what, then monitor logs to see what actually happens.

M17
The SEO Content System
Stop publishing random posts
AARON
Most people skip straight to "what should I blog about." That question is downstream of a more important one: what are you trying to rank for, and does that page already exist? The three-layer model below is the answer. Figure out which bucket a piece belongs to before you write a single word.

A modern SEO system has three layers: money pages, authority pages, and proof pages.

Money pages
Service, product, location, comparison, pricing, and demo pages. These convert demand.
Authority pages
Guides, definitions, frameworks, research, templates, calculators, and industry reports. These earn trust and citations.
Proof pages
Case studies, reviews, testimonials, before/after examples, teardown posts, benchmarks. These make claims believable.

AI search raises the value of authority and proof pages because answer engines need sources that make claims easier to support.

M19

Local trust becomes machine-readable proof

For local businesses, SEO is not just the website. It is the website, Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect, reviews, local citations, service area clarity, photos, menus/services/products, local landing pages, and reputation signals.

AssetWhat to optimize

ReviewsVolume, freshness, specificity, response quality, service/location keywords naturally mentioned by customers.

AI answers about local businesses will lean on whatever structured and semi-structured proof is easiest to verify. Keep the public facts clean.

M20
Ecommerce SEO
Products need facts, not fluff

Ecommerce search is especially vulnerable to AI summarization because users ask comparison and fit questions: best product for a use case, cheapest reliable option, difference between models, sizing, compatibility, return policies, and reviews.

Product pages

Make facts complete

Specs, variants, compatibility, availability, shipping, returns, images, video, FAQs.

Category pages

Help decisions

Buying guides, filters, comparisons, top use cases, expert picks, review summaries.

Reviews

Surface real experience

Use review snippets ethically. Highlight verified buyer patterns.

Feeds

Keep data clean

Merchant feeds, product schema, price, stock, GTINs, and variant identifiers.

M21
Programmatic SEO Without Making Trash
Scale pages only when each page deserves to exist

Programmatic SEO still works. But AI search makes thin scaled pages easier to ignore than ever — because an AI can just answer the question instead of sending the traffic. If you create 2,000 pages from a template, each page needs unique data, local proof, user value, or a real reason to exist. The threshold for "worthwhile page" just got higher.

Good
Unique inventory, location data, pricing, availability, comparisons, examples, reviews, maps, or calculators.
Bad
Same paragraph with city/product swapped. Doorway pages. AI-generated filler with no proof.
Test
Would a user be satisfied landing directly on this page, or would they immediately search again?

M23
Topical Authority
Own the topic, not just the keyword

Topical authority means your site covers a subject deeply enough that users and systems can trust you across the whole problem space. This is not just publishing many articles. It is building a coherent knowledge system.

1

Define the topic map

Core concepts, problems, alternatives, tools, mistakes, costs, examples, and FAQs.

2

Create hub pages

Big guides that organize the topic and link to deeper pages.

3

Add proof pages

Case studies, teardown posts, benchmarks, calculators, and examples.

4

Interlink intentionally

Pages should reinforce each other, not compete randomly.

M24
Brand Search Is The Moat
When users ask for you by name, algorithms have less ambiguity

Generic search is crowded. Brand search is clearer. A strong brand creates navigational demand, direct traffic, mentions, reviews, repeat visits, and trust signals that are harder to copy than a blog template.

Build memory

Repeatable concepts

Name your frameworks, templates, tools, and signature processes.

Build proof

Show outcomes

Case studies, public examples, testimonials, screenshots, and before/after evidence.

Build distribution

Leave the website

YouTube, LinkedIn, podcasts, newsletters, communities, GitHub, directories.

Build entity clarity

Be consistent

Same name, bios, handles, descriptions, logos, and links across the web.

M25
Reviews, Communities, And UGC
AI search loves the messy human web, but you cannot fake it safely

Forums, Reddit, review sites, YouTube comments, community posts, and customer reviews often influence AI summaries because they contain lived experience. You should not manipulate them. You should earn real discussion by doing useful work, asking for reviews ethically, responding well, and participating where your audience already is.

DON'T
Do not create fake reviews, fake Reddit threads, fake comparison pages, or fake third-party endorsements. AI systems may ingest them, but humans and platforms eventually punish fraud. Short-term visibility is not worth long-term trust loss.

Instead: build review request flows, answer community questions, publish transparent comparisons, and document real customer outcomes.

M26
Refresh And Decay
Old pages lose trust when the world changes around them

In AI search, stale pages are risky because summaries need current facts. Refresh cycles should focus on pages where freshness changes the answer: pricing, tools, laws, model capabilities, product comparisons, statistics, screenshots, and best-practice recommendations.

Refresh monthly
Fast-moving AI, software, pricing, regulatory, and benchmark pages.
Refresh quarterly
Core service pages, comparisons, local pages, top guides.
Refresh yearly
Evergreen definitions and stable tutorials, unless traffic drops.
Do not fake
Changing the date without meaningful updates is not freshness.

The 90-Day SEO + AI Search Plan
What to do first when the map is big
1

Days 1-15: Baseline

Set up Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, analytics, sitemap, robots review, crawl audit, top page inventory, and AI prompt baseline.

2

Days 16-30: Technical cleanup

Fix index blockers, canonicals, broken links, internal linking gaps, slow templates, duplicate pages, and missing metadata.

3

Days 31-45: Entity foundation

Improve About, author bios, organization schema, service pages, location facts, sameAs profiles, and review flows.

4

Days 46-65: Content rebuild

Upgrade top pages with direct answers, proof blocks, tables, examples, schema, media, internal links, and clear CTAs.

5

Days 66-80: Authority push

6

END
The future-proof SEO strategy is not chasing every algorithm update. It is building a site that search engines can crawl, answer engines can safely cite, and humans can trust enough to actually do something. Everything else is tactics.

M31
Keyword Research SOP For VAs
How to collect and organize keywords without pretending to be the strategist

The VA's job is not to pick the whole strategy alone. The VA's job is to gather clean evidence so the strategist or owner can decide faster.

1

Start from existing data

Export Search Console queries for the last 3 months. Include query, page, clicks, impressions, CTR, and position.

2

Group by intent

Label each keyword as informational, commercial, local, transactional, support, branded, or competitor.

3

Find page match

Mark whether the site already has a page for the keyword. If yes, paste the URL. If no, mark "gap."

4

Check the SERP manually

Search the keyword in an incognito window. Note what ranks: guides, local pack, videos, tools, category pages, forums, AI Overview.

5

Collect competitor examples

Save the top 3 useful competitor URLs and write one sentence on why each page satisfies the query.

Publishing SOP
How to publish without quietly damaging SEO

Publishing is where many SEO mistakes happen: wrong slug, missing canonical, giant images, no internal links, broken formatting, missing author, copied metadata, or accidentally noindexing the page.

1

Before upload

Confirm approved title, URL slug, author, category, publish date, target keyword, CTA, and internal links.

2

During upload

Format headings, compress images, add alt text, add tables, add schema, add meta title/description.

3

Before publish

Preview desktop and mobile. Test links. Check noindex is off. Check canonical is correct.

4

After publish

Add internal links from 2-5 relevant old pages. Submit URL in Search Console if appropriate. Log in tracker.

AI Search Visibility SOP
How a VA checks if answer engines see you

AI visibility tracking is immature, so support teams need a simple prompt set and a consistent logging process. The goal is not perfect science. The goal is directional visibility: who gets cited, what claims appear, and which content gaps repeat.

1

Create 10-25 saved prompts

Use real customer questions, comparison prompts, local prompts, and buying-intent prompts.

2

Run monthly

Use the same prompts in ChatGPT search, Google AI features when available, Copilot/Bing, and any relevant answer engine.

3

Record citations

Log whether your brand/page appears, which competitors appear, and what source URLs are cited.

4

Extract gaps

If competitors are cited, note what their page has that yours lacks: data, table, definition, case study, comparison, author proof.
