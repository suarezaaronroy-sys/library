01 · Thesis

What GHL Actually Is

Not a CRM. A marketing engine wearing a CRM's clothes — and knowing the difference is the whole job.

Most people list "GoHighLevel" under CRM skills. That's the first tell that they don't really run it. GHL is marketing software that grew a CRM to keep you from leaving. Build with that grain and it's the best value in the category; ask it to be Salesforce and it disappoints you in slow, expensive ways.

Integrations & perimeter

The hub it was born to be

Native ad-lead ingestion, Stripe, calendars, telephony, and webhooks out to Zapier / Make / n8n. Happiest as the center of a wheel, not the whole machine.

Funnel & site building

Capture surfaces, fast

Funnels, forms, and sites that wire straight into the database. Templated once, cloned in thirty minutes. Where speed compounds.

Marketing data capture

Every touch, attributed

UTMs, hidden fields, trigger links, call tracking — the raw material of knowing which dollar produced which customer. Collected well even when reported poorly.

Lead management

Speed-to-lead, pipelines, follow-up

Instant response, pipeline movement, nurture, reactivation. The unsexy plumbing that decides whether spend becomes revenue or evaporates.

↻ Honest assessment — and the question you should ask me

GHL is mediocre at deep reporting, its data model is rigid next to a real CRM, and its shared sending infrastructure means your deliverability partly rides on strangers. So the fair pushback is: "then why GHL instead of HubSpot or a proper stack?" Because for a service business or agency it consolidates five-to-ten tools, white-labels, costs a fraction at scale, and clones a proven build via snapshots. You buy consolidation and speed — and you bolt on n8n and a real BI layer exactly where GHL runs out of room. Knowing where it runs out is the job.

E1 · Mandate

The Admin Mandate

What I own end to end — and why it's one person, not three.

As the administrator I own five surfaces. When one person owns all five, leads stop falling between an agency, a VA, and "whoever set it up."

  1. The instance — sub-accounts, users, roles, permissions, billing hygiene, and the naming conventions everyone else follows.
  2. The data model — custom fields, custom objects, and a strict tag taxonomy that keeps the database queryable a year out.
  3. The marketing engine — funnels, forms, calendars, email / SMS / WhatsApp, and the campaigns on them.
  4. The automation layer — every workflow that moves a lead without a human touching it.
  5. The integration perimeter — ads, payments, telephony, and the n8n / Zapier / webhook connections to everything GHL doesn't do natively.
⚡ Power Tip

The first value isn't a new funnel — it's consolidated ownership. Split ownership means every handoff is a leak. I close the org-chart leaks before building a single new asset.

Operating principles

PrincipleWhat it means in the build
Single source of truthGHL holds the canonical contact record. Integrations sync to it, never fork it.
Speed-to-leadFirst automated response inside 5 minutes, always. The first responder usually wins.
No orphan leadsEvery contact belongs to a pipeline stage or a nurture. Nothing sits untagged and untouched.
IdempotencyRe-running a workflow can't double-text, double-charge, or duplicate a contact.
Instrument firstTracking ships before the funnel goes live. If it isn't measured, it didn't happen.
E2 · Perimeter

Integrations & the Capture Perimeter

GHL is the hub. The perimeter is where the real work routes — native where solid, n8n where logic gets real.

E3 · Capture

Funnels & Capture Surfaces

Every funnel has the same seven joints. Most break on the back four.

#StageThe job of this stage
1OfferA reason to raise a hand worth more than the effort to claim it
2TrafficThe right people arriving — paid, organic, referral, owned
3CaptureContact info exchanged for the offer, friction minimized
4NurtureTrust built across touches until they're ready
5CloseThe booked call, quote, or checkout
6RetainOnboarding and repeat that raise lifetime value
7ReferHappy customers turned into the cheapest traffic there is

Build a capture funnel that doesn't leak

  1. One promise above the fold — no second ask competing with it.
  2. Form wired direct to pipeline: 2 visible fields max, hidden UTM fields, consent checkbox (never pre-ticked).
  3. Proof close behind the promise — reviews, results, a guarantee.
  4. Tag on submission source:website and fire the Day-0 sequence inside 5 minutes.
  5. Test with a dummy contact — watch the workflow History tab execute end to end before going live.
The #1 silent funnel bug: a submit button not linked to a form block. It looks fine and captures nothing. Every funnel I ship gets a live test submission before it's called done.
E4 · Lead management

Speed-to-Lead & Lead Management

The highest-ROI automation in the build — and where the receipts come from.

Most "we need more leads" problems are actually "we lose half the leads we have" problems. The fix is automated, ships in week one, and pays for the engagement before anything fancy gets built.

Trigger: Form Submitted / Ad lead received
  0-60 sec:  Auto-SMS + auto-email  (warm before a human blinks)
  +0 sec:   Create opportunity -> stage: New Lead
  +0 sec:   Internal notification to the rep
  On reply: Stop the sequence, route to a human, tag status:engaged

Stages are defined by buyer action — New Lead, Contacted, Booked, Showed, Won/Lost — so the board reflects reality. Leads that aren't ready get a 7–12 touch nurture that stops the instant they convert. And the fastest ROI of all: database reactivation against the cold list already paid for.

↻ Receipts — this isn't theory

On a dual-market rental build (G002) the engine produced a 34.2% open rate and 18 consultations from a 340-contact cold list. On a construction CRM (G001) it ran 76 custom fields and 24 automations, QA-verified through 13 documented corrections. The front half of GHL is the part I've shipped and measured — which is why I can promise it before I've seen your account.

E5 · Data

Marketing Data & Attribution

If you can't trace a closed deal to its source, every budget call is a guess.

ParameterExample values
utm_sourcefacebook, google, newsletter
utm_mediumcpc, email, social
utm_campaignthe specific ad or campaign name

UTMs on every link, captured into hidden form fields, enforced as a source field on every contact. The standard I hold: any lead, any day, traceable from first click to closed revenue.

↻ Honest assessment — GHL reporting runs out of room here

GHL's native dashboard answers "how many, from where, what cost, what's it worth" — and not much past that. So I pipe the data out: GHL -> Google Sheets -> Looker Studio, where the owner gets a branded two-minute read instead of twelve charts they ignore. One north-star metric (CAC or booked revenue) at the top; everything else ladders up. Weekly leading indicators, monthly lagging, quarterly reset.

02 · Discovery

Where It Varies — Your Business

Read these as the intake I'd run in our first working session. Notice the shift: you're answering a consultant's discovery now, not interviewing a candidate.

The intake — your answers go here

  1. Niche & industry? ____________ — sets funnel archetype, compliance surface (e.g. HIPAA), and the sales cycle the nurture has to survive.
  2. Greenfield or rebuild? ____________ — a blank account is a clean build; an inherited one starts with an audit and a lead-leak map before I touch anything.
  3. One business, multi-location, or agency / multiple clients? ____________ — this sets the entire architecture: single account vs. sub-accounts vs. snapshots and SaaS mode.
  4. What happens after capture? ____________ — your sales motion and fulfillment, the half of the funnel the engine hands off to.
  5. What's the offer, and what's a customer worth? ____________ — sets the ROI math below and tells me how hard each stage has to convert.

How each answer reshapes the build

Single business

One account, depth over breadth

Rigorous tagging, one polished funnel per offer, deep nurture. Simplest architecture, fastest to optimize.

Multi-location

Sub-accounts + shared snapshot

One master snapshot cloned per location, location-routed booking, roll-up reporting. Consistency without rebuilding each.

Agency / multiple

Snapshots, rebilling, SaaS mode

White-label, a vetted template library, per-client sub-accounts, rebilling above modeled cost. The build becomes a product.

Greenfield vs. rebuild

Build forward vs. stop the bleeding

Greenfield: straight to the engine. Rebuild: a 14-day audit, a lead-leak map with dollar estimates, and quick wins that fund the rest.

⚡ Power Tip — the question most candidates never ask

"How many businesses are we building this for?" is the single most architecture-defining question in GHL, and almost nobody asks it in an interview. Asking it signals I think in GHL's actual operating model — sub-accounts, snapshots, SaaS — not just "I can build a funnel."

04 · Economics

Economics & the Ask

The case isn't "marketing matters." It's arithmetic.

Take a business at 100 inquiries a month, closing 20% at a $1,200 average ticket — that's $288k/year in won work. Recover even a quarter of the leads currently leaking at first-response and follow-up, and at the same close rate that's roughly $70k+ of recovered revenue from leads already paid for — before a dollar of new ad spend. (Illustrative; in Q1 I replace them with yours.)

The ROI logic. A GHL admin pays for themselves in recovered leakage alone. Better funnels, lower CAC, retention, and referral are upside that compounds quarter over quarter. The system is an appreciating asset, not a recurring cost.

Give me a sandbox sub-account and one working session, and I'll build you a live capture funnel + speed-to-lead workflow before we finish talking. Then the first 30 days to diagnose and the next 60 to prove it. By day 90: recovered revenue, a system you can see, and a roadmap earned by evidence.
