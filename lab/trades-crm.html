The problem · where trades businesses leak money

Missed calls

A ringing phone on a job site is a lost lead. No call-back, no text, no record it ever happened.

→ capture gap

Slow quotes

Days between survey and sent quote. The keenest lead cools while the paperwork waits.

→ convert gap

No follow-up

Unsigned quotes sit untouched. Most jobs are won on the second or third nudge that never gets sent.

→ nurture gap

Aged debt

Work delivered, invoice forgotten. Cash the business already earned sits uncollected past 30 days.

→ collect gap

Silent after

Job done, customer gone. No review ask, no maintenance reminder, no reactivation. Reputation and repeat work left on the table.

→ retain gap

The automations that run it

Tripwires & loops

  1. Missed-call → instant text-back + callback task
  2. Quote sent → 48h follow-up → decision reminder → stop on reply
  3. No-show detected → auto-reschedule loop (max 2)
  4. Deposit cleared → schedule crew + order materials
  5. Job complete → warranty PDF + compliance file + review ask
  6. Invoice overdue → staged chase (7/14/30d) → escalate to owner
  7. Estimate > threshold → manager approval before send (HiTL)
  8. Insurance / cert within 30 days of expiry → owner task
  9. Lead idle past stage SLA → escalate / reassign
  10. Cooled lead → 12-month reactivation nurture

Where it actually gets built · demo vs real

Platform

GoHighLevel carries CRM core, pipelines, comms, calendars, and workflow automations. The map above becomes pipelines, custom fields, and workflow triggers — configured, not coded.

Integrations

Stripe / bank for deposits and payments, accounting sync for job costing and aged debt, call tracking for capture, e-sign for quotes. Each is a wired dependency, not HTML.

Human in the loop

HiTL gates hold anything irreversible — quotes over threshold, escalations, owner approvals — so automation speeds the work without removing judgement from the money decisions.
