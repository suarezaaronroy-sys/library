This site exists because I kept needing my own notes. Payment processing taught me that undocumented knowledge walks out the door with people; CRM work taught me that systems nobody can explain aren't systems. So I write the manual for everything — first for myself, as working reference, and then published openly because gatekeeping operational knowledge has never once made an operation better.

The result is the library: seventeen grimoires, from a 76-field production CRM to a three-volume series on how money moves, how work moves, and why organizations behave — plus field manuals on Claude and Cowork, a 30-chapter guide built for Filipino VAs entering remote work, and field notes on the quiet systems behind ordinary work. It doubles as my portfolio, but that's the side effect. The primary function is the same one it had on day one — it's where I look things up.

The pattern underneath all the work: most operational breakdowns aren't effort problems, they're structural. Work that depends on specific people. Tools that don't connect. Knowledge that lives in heads. I find those structures and rebuild them so they survive without heroics.

How I think

My first question is rarely "what should I do?" — it's "how does this work?" Root causes, dependencies, failure modes, incentives. Once the mechanism is clear, the fix usually writes itself, and the details I didn't memorize can be reconstructed from the model. That's why the same approach keeps working across CRM systems, payment processing, marketing engines, organizational design, and the occasional motorcycle — they're all variations of one puzzle.

I rate competence over appearances — titles and polish tell me little; whether the thing works tells me everything. I prefer efficiency over drama, and I'd rather fix a problem than discuss it a fourth time. The library is free for a related reason: I value money as removed constraints, not displayed success, and gatekeeping operational knowledge has never once made an operation better.

Off the clock: motorcycles — mechanical honesty, machines that do what they claim — and fiction that keeps circling the same themes as the case files: systems controlling people, hidden infrastructure, reality versus perception. Grimoire 011 is what happens when those themes stop pretending to be fiction.
