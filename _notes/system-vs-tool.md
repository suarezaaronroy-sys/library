A tool is capability. A system is capability plus the decisions around it: who owns each field, what each stage means, what happens on every trigger, who fixes it when it drifts. The vendor sells the first part. Nobody can sell you the second — it has to be designed against how your operation actually behaves.

This is why two companies on identical software get opposite results, and why "we have a CRM" tells me nothing about whether you have a working revenue system. The tool was never the system. The structure around it is.
