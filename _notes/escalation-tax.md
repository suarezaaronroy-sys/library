Every escalation looks like a five-minute favor. It isn't. The senior person pays the five minutes, plus the context they dropped, plus the re-entry time into deep work — and the asker pays the wait. Ten escalations a day is routinely three-plus hours of your most expensive attention, spent answering questions the system should have answered.

The tax is invisible because nobody invoices it. Track one week of escalations by topic and two patterns appear immediately: most questions repeat, and most repeats trace to a missing definition, a missing default, or a missing owner — all structural, all fixable.
