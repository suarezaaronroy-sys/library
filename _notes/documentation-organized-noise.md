The wiki exists. The SOPs exist. Someone spent a weekend organizing the folder structure. And the team still asks the senior person, because asking takes ten seconds and finding takes ten minutes — when it works at all.

Documentation has exactly one test: can the person doing the work reach the right answer at the moment of work, faster than interrupting a human? Fail that test and the docs are organized noise — accurate, complete, and irrelevant. Passing it is mostly about placement, not prose: the checklist inside the workflow, the definition on the field, the SOP linked from the task. Write less. Position better.
