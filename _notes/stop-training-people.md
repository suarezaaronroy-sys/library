When the same questions keep arriving at the same senior people, the standard diagnosis is a training problem. Run the workshop, update the onboarding deck, hope it sticks. It rarely does — because the questions were never caused by missing training. They were caused by an environment that gives people nowhere to turn except up.

The distinction that changes how you build: **training transfers knowledge to a person; systems make knowledge available at the moment of work.** Trained knowledge decays, leaves with the employee, and bottlenecks at whoever holds it. Systematized knowledge — the checklist in the workflow, the definition in the field label, the SOP linked from the task itself — survives turnover and answers questions before they're asked.
